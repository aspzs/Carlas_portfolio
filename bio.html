    • about

    • Carla Schwering was born on
      March 31, 1997 in Vienna, Austria.

    • me

    • After her double high-schooldegree at the German-French-school in Munich, Germany in 2015 and several internships, she enrolled in theatre studies and art history (BA) at the LMU in 2016.

      This period of study was accompanied by a one-year permanent assistantship at the Residenztheater.
      With the completion of her Bachelor in 2019, she decided to do something more practical and artistic in relation to that art form and so she applied for the diploma course in stage and costume design, film and exhibition architecture at the Mozarteum in Salzburg, Austria.

      She started her studies in autumn 2019 and since then she has had several interesting practical experiences.

      Among others, she was an intern at Burgtheater in Vienna in the costume department in September-October 2020, assisted the freelance photographer Olaf Unverzart in Munich in November-December 2020 and completed an internship in the cultural sector at the Goethe Institute in Paris, France in September-October 2022.
      At the university she worked on several own theatre and costume projects having their final shows in and around Salzburg.

      Carla has always had an interest in photography and film, as they can be used independently as well as in the context of theatre.
      She is particularly fascinated by how the media can be used creatively: for example changing photos afterwards and intervening in them, like on stage or in a film set, where you transform the real space and consciously use the dream and fiction.
      Carla likes to approach her projects theoretically on a base of research, to then conceptually choose the right medium and find a metaphorical and poetical access and translation to what she wants to express.
